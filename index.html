Ada Lovelace (1815-1852)

"Imagination is the discovering faculty, pre-eminently. It is that which penetrates into the unseen worlds around us, the worlds of Science."

Ada Lovelace, an English mathematician (Anne Isabelle Milbanke) and daughter of the poet (Lord Byron).
She believed that computers could become much more than calculators.

Ada in History

  • Lovelace worked with Charles Babbage, the “father of the computer".
  • Ada wrote the first algorithm for a machine and is considered the first computer programmer.
  • Ada invented looping in computer programming.
  • Ada was the first person to envision the incredible potential of universal computation.
  • Ada's notes anticipated future developments, including computer-generated music.
